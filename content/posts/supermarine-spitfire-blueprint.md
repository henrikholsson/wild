## I. General Description

The Supermarine Spitfire Mark IX is a single-seat, low-wing monoplane fighter aircraft of stressed-skin, all-metal construction. Developed as a direct response to the Focke-Wulf Fw 190, the Mk.IX incorporates the Rolls-Royce Merlin 61-series engine with two-stage supercharger, providing markedly improved performance at altitude over previous variants.

The airframe retains the characteristic elliptical wing planform of the Spitfire series, optimised for low induced drag at high angles of attack.

## V. Powerplant

    ENGINE                  Rolls-Royce Merlin 61
    TYPE                    V-12, liquid-cooled, supercharged
    DISPLACEMENT            27 litres      (1,649 cu in)
    SUPERCHARGER            Two-stage, two-speed
    RATED POWER             1,565 hp at 12,250 ft
    FUEL                    100/130 octane
    COOLANT                 70% water / 30% glycol

## VI. Performance

    MAX SPEED               408 mph at 25,000 ft
    CRUISE SPEED            324 mph at 20,000 ft
    SERVICE CEILING         43,000 ft
    RATE OF CLIMB           3,860 ft/min
    RANGE                   434 miles (internal fuel)
    RANGE (DROP TANK)       980 miles
